Broadcast engineers are responsible for the strength, clarity, and overall quality of sounds and images broadcast on radio and television, and—increasingly—sound and image transmitted via satellite, cable, and the internet.

Broadcast engineers install, operate, maintain and repair the equipment used to make and broadcast radio and television programmes. They make sure that programmes are broadcast to the highest quality and on time.

Broadcast engineers set up studio equipment. install multimedia hardware, software and digital broadcast technology systems. set up and operate links between studios and outside broadcast (OB) units. edit programmes live as they're being transmitted or recorded.
